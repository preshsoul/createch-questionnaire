Founder Perspectives on Personal Branding in African Createch

Participant Information & Consent

Thank you for agreeing to be part of this study.

This questionnaire forms the primary data collection instrument for an MBA dissertation examining how founders of African Createch startups build and manage their personal brands on digital platforms — and how those efforts shape how audiences perceive, trust, and engage with their ventures.

The questions are deliberately open. There are no right or wrong answers. What matters is your honest, reflective account — written the way you would speak if we were sitting across from each other.

The questionnaire has five sections and takes approximately 10–15 minutes. Each section is anchored to a specific research question, so your time is spent only on what is analytically necessary.

Your responses are automatically stored in a secure encrypted database. You will be assigned a pseudonym and no identifying details will appear in the final research report. Your email, if provided, is stored separately and will never appear in the analysis.

Identity, Strategy, and Self-Presentation

RQ1 · ~12 min

This section addresses the first research question: how do African Createch founders perceive and strategically implement their personal branding? Questions are grounded in Self-Presentation Theory (Goffman, 1959) and Social Identity Theory (Tajfel & Turner, 1979).

RQ1 · Self-Presentation Theory B1. When you are active online as a founder — posting, engaging, sharing — what are you consciously trying to communicate about who you are? Is there a version of yourself you are building, protecting, or amplifying?
Think about the impression you want people to form — not just what you post, but the identity underneath the posts. Is the version of you that exists publicly an accurate reflection of who you are privately, or is it a curated construct?

Signalling, Trust, and Your Startup's Reputation

RQ2 · ~10 min

This section addresses the second research question: how does founder personal branding influence the digital brand equity of their startup? Questions draw on Signalling Theory (Spence, 1973) and the Resource-Based View (Barney, 1991).

RQ2 · Signalling Theory · Brand Awareness C1. Has something you said or did publicly ever translated into a tangible shift in how people found or responded to your startup?
Think about inbound inquiries, investor interest, partnership requests, or new customers arriving because of your visibility rather than the startup's own marketing. What was the signal being received? What did people infer about the startup from what they saw of you?
